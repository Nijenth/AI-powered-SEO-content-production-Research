# Sources: AI-Powered SEO Content Production

This document lists the experts selected for this research project, why each one was chosen, and links to their primary channels.

**Selection criteria:** Each person had to be a genuine *practitioner* — someone who has built, run, or shipped real content/SEO systems at scale (not just commentators or generic "AI guru" accounts). Preference was given to people with verifiable track records (companies built, revenue figures, named clients, published methodologies) over follower count alone.

---

## 1. Eli Schwartz
**Role:** Growth advisor, author of *Product-Led SEO*
**LinkedIn:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/5le/
**Newsletter:** Product-Led SEO (eliischwartz.co)

**Why he's on this list:** Schwartz's core thesis — that the most defensible SEO strategy is building pages that are genuinely useful as *products* (calculators, comparison tools, live data) rather than just optimized content — has become directly relevant to the AI search era. His argument is that product-driven pages resist being summarized away by LLMs and therefore earn disproportionate AI citations. Strategic-level thinking on what content survives AI search, not just tactical AI-writing advice.

**Annotated, added:** 2026-06-20

## Notes on methodology

- All 10 sources were identified and cross-verified via web search rather than relying on existing "top X" listicles alone — several names from generic listicles (e.g., general business influencers, pure SEO-tool marketing pages, anonymous agency case studies) were deliberately excluded because they didn't meet the practitioner bar.
- The panel deliberately spans different angles on the same topic: tactical SEO operators (Gotch, Niezgoda), strategic/distribution thinkers (Balfour, Schwartz), technical/engineering depth (King, Tuğberk GÜBÜR), data-driven experimentalists (Shepard, Indig), and a quality-control counterweight (Solis) plus an audience-precision counterpoint (Law) — rather than 10 people saying the same thing.
- LinkedIn posts were intentionally *not* scraped for this research, in line with LinkedIn's Terms of Service. Where LinkedIn content from these experts is referenced, it is manually collected, summarized, and linked back to the original — not reproduced in full.
