## Why this piece matters for this research

This is the single most comprehensive, practical, and data-backed framework found across all 10 experts in this research set. Where most other sources explain concepts or share opinions, Solis provides an actual operational workflow — a 12-step checklist most directly usable as the backbone of a future "AI-powered SEO content production" playbook.

## The core reframe

Solis's central argument: AI search optimization should not start with "let's make more AI-friendly content." It should start with defining which AI search journeys you want to influence, diagnosing where visibility gaps actually come from, and only then taking targeted action — because different visibility gaps require completely different fixes (a content gap, a technical accessibility gap, and a third-party trust gap all look similar from the outside but need different solutions).

## The 12-step framework (condensed)

1. **Define the prompts/journeys that matter** — build a prompt library from real buyer language (sales transcripts, reviews, support logs), not a keyword tool. Weight toward non-branded, commercial, and comparison prompts, not just branded ones.
2. **Measure current AI presence** — track five KPIs: prompt coverage, recommendation rate, linked citation rate, comparative win rate, and representation accuracy — separately per platform (don't blend ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, AI Overviews into one score).
3. **Diagnose the gap before fixing** — a visibility gap can stem from very different causes (technical access, weak content, third-party corroboration, inconsistent entity signals); the fix differs by cause.
4. **Make priority pages retrievable and extractable** — audit AI crawler access (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, etc.), ensure content isn't trapped in fragile JavaScript, and structure pages so individual sections can stand alone as "chunks."
5. **Build decision-support content, not just informational coverage** — pages need to answer "who is this for/not for," show trade-offs, comparisons, and proof — not just explain a topic.
6. **Earn the citation and the click separately** — these are different jobs. Her own research found brand-entry pages captured 57.7% of AI traffic but only 3.0% of AI citations — citation-worthy content (original research, data, methodology) and traffic-converting content are not the same pages.
7. **Align entity, naming and positioning signals** — consistency of brand/product description across owned site, schema, social profiles, review platforms, and directories.
8. **Work the third-party source ecosystem** — map which sources (review sites, forums, directories, YouTube, partner pages) actually shape AI answers for your prompts, and treat digital PR as part of AI search optimization rather than a separate workstream.
9. **Make commercial information machine-readable** — pricing, plans, product specs need to be explicit and structured, not hidden behind sales calls or PDFs.
10. **Localize by market, not just language** — her research across 10 markets found the number of domains needed to capture 50% of AI clicks varied by 10x between verticals (~5 domains in ecommerce vs. ~47 in travel); a US/English playbook doesn't transfer directly.
11. **Report without overclaiming** — separate observed, proxy, and modelled signals. Her data point: across 40 sites analyzed, organic search was ~108x larger than AI referral traffic in April 2026 — a useful corrective against AI-search hype.
12. **Run a recurring validation loop** — re-test the same prompt groups over time rather than one-off audits; validate that specific changes actually moved specific metrics.

## A closing principle worth flagging directly

Solis explicitly warns against gaming AI systems — prompt injection, fabricated reviews, manufactured community signals — arguing these exploit current blind spots that get patched, trading durable trust for fragile, short-term visibility. This is a useful ethical/practical guardrail to carry into any playbook built from this research.
