## Why this piece matters for this research

Mike King is the most technically grounded voice in this research set — his framework, "Relevance Engineering," is the closest thing to a genuine engineering discipline (as opposed to a marketing tactic) applied to AI-era content visibility. He's also unusually credible on the "production" half of this topic specifically: iPullRank has been experimenting with language models and building tools that mimicked AI Overviews internally since as early as 2020, well before this became an industry-wide concern.

## What "Relevance Engineering" actually is

King defines it as a discipline sitting at the intersection of five fields: information retrieval, artificial intelligence, user experience, content strategy, and digital PR. He frames it explicitly as channel-agnostic — not "SEO with an AI twist," but a genuinely new operating system for visibility across however users now discover and evaluate information (search engines, AI chatbots, AI Overviews, agentic search).

Practically, the discipline focuses on:
- **Embedding-aware optimization** — understanding how content is represented in vector space, not just what keywords it contains
- **Retrieval precision** — how content performs in dense and hybrid retrieval systems (the actual mechanism AI search uses to pull relevant passages)
- **Passage/chunk-level relevance** — moving the unit of optimization from "the page" to "the passage," since AI systems retrieve and cite fragments, not whole pages
- **Synthetic query optimization** — AI Overviews and chatbots generate their own internal "synthetic queries" to research an answer before responding; King argues part of the discipline is now optimizing for those hidden, AI-generated queries, not just the user's literal typed query

## The production-relevant argument

King's most directly relevant point for "AI-powered SEO content production" specifically: he argues SEOs need to move beyond blog posts into white papers and original research to actually hold up under this new paradigm, and that practitioners are no longer "mechanics tweaking websites" but need to function as engineers building entire systems — moving toward agentic, automated, modular content systems that adapt to visibility shifts rather than static, one-off content campaigns.

He's also explicit that success metrics need to change accordingly — branded visibility, mindshare, and directional traffic signals, rather than only rankings or click volume.

## An open, community-facing extension

As of mid-2025, King began building RelevanceEngineering.org as an open-source, community-driven framework repository — including a proposed "gateway specification" for data portability across SEO/crawling tools, explicitly because he considers vendor lock-in on crawl data a structural problem for the industry. This is a notable signal: he's not just consulting on the framework privately, he's trying to make it an open standard.
