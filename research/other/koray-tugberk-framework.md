## Why this piece matters for this research

Koray is the most methodologically rigorous and idiosyncratic practitioner in this set — he's built a named, proprietary framework ("Koray's Framework," nicknamed "Koraynese" by his own community) used across 700+ client engagements since 2015, including Fortune 500 companies. Unlike most SEO content advice, his approach is explicitly rooted in computational linguistics (entity-attribute-value modeling, frame semantics, lexical semantics) rather than generic best practices.

## The core methodology: Topical Authority via Semantic Content Networks

Koray's foundational claim, predating the current AI-search moment by years: topical authority — not backlink volume — is the primary ranking determinant in modern search. His method builds this through:

- **Topical maps** — a structured hierarchy of all topics, subtopics, and their semantic relationships within a niche, built before any content is written
- **Entity-Attribute-Value (EAV) coverage** — systematically mapping what entities exist in a topic space and what attributes/values need to be covered for completeness
- **Semantic Content Networks** — content connected not just by hyperlinks but by semantic relationships (predicates connecting entities), designed to be easier for search engines' NLP systems to process and associate

## The 41 authorship rules

A set of granular, sentence-and-paragraph-level writing rules designed for maximum alignment with how NLP systems parse content. Notable examples:
- **One macro context per page** — each page should have a single, clear semantic focus rather than mixing topics
- **Question-format H2 headings paired with ~40-word extractive answers** — directly engineered to be extracted as featured snippets / AI Overview answers
- **Hub-and-spoke internal linking** structured around topical and entity relationships, not just generic "related posts" linking

## Koray's Agents — the AI production layer

This is the most directly relevant piece for "AI-powered SEO content production" specifically: Koray built custom GPT-based agents to operationalize his own framework at scale. By his own account, these agents:
- Significantly accelerated his team's workflow and improved output accuracy
- Reduced the need for constant human communication/coordination on complex SEO tasks
- Are explicitly *not* simple NER (named-entity recognition) or lemmatization tools, which he characterizes as the typical shallow approach most "SEO GPTs" take — his agents are built to understand and apply the deeper semantic methodology itself, not just process text mechanically

This distinction matters: many people building "AI SEO content tools" are automating surface-level tasks (keyword insertion, basic NLP checks). Koray's agents are reportedly built to encode and apply an entire proprietary content strategy framework — a meaningfully more ambitious (and harder to replicate) approach to AI content production.
